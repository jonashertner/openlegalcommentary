# Art. 196 BV

## Case Law

The case law on Art. 196 BV demonstrates the practical application of the various transitional provisions by the courts. The decisions focus primarily on the time-limited collection of direct federal tax, the obligation for mutual recognition of educational qualifications, and procedural aspects of the major railway projects.

### Time Limitation of Direct Federal Tax (Art. 196 No. 13 BV)

**BGE 142 II 182** of 1 January 2016
The Federal Supreme Court clarified the territorial jurisdiction for federal tax collection in case of change of canton and emphasized the constitutional foundations of direct federal tax.
The decision confirms the importance of Art. 196 No. 13 BV for the time limitation of the tax collection authority.

> «The Confederation has the authority, currently limited until the end of 2035 (Art. 42 para. 1 in conjunction with Art. 128 and Art. 196 No. 13 BV), to levy a 'direct tax' (thus Art. 128 para. 1 BV). This is 'to be assessed and collected by the cantons'.»

**BGE 151 II 101** of 1 January 2025  
The Federal Supreme Court clarified the reversal in cases of incompetent collection of direct federal tax and referred to the constitutional time limitation.
The decision shows the practical effects of the «mandatory law» of the cantons for tax collection within the framework of the Confederation's time-limited competence.

> «The Confederation has the authority, currently limited until the end of 2035 (Art. 42 para. 1 in conjunction with Art. 128 and Art. 196 No. 13 BV), to levy a 'direct tax' (thus Art. 128 para. 1 BV).»

### Mutual Recognition of Educational Qualifications (Art. 196 No. 5 BV)

**BGE 130 I 26** of 27 November 2003  
The Federal Supreme Court examined admission restrictions for service providers to practice at the expense of mandatory health insurance under the aspect of the transitional provision to Art. 95 BV.
The decision clarifies the scope of the transitional obligation for mutual recognition of educational qualifications.

> «According to Art. 95 para. 2 sentence 2 BV, the Confederation guarantees that persons with scientific training or a federal, cantonal or cantonally recognised educational qualification may practice their profession throughout Switzerland. Until the enactment of the corresponding federal legislation, the cantons are obliged to mutually recognise educational qualifications (Art. 196 No. 5 BV).»

### Major Railway Projects and NEAT (Art. 196 No. 3 BV)

**BGE 131 II 581** of 1 January 2005  
The Federal Supreme Court decided on a railway law planning approval procedure for a detailed project of the New Railway Link through the Alps (NEAT).
The decision shows the practical implementation of the constitutionally anchored major railway projects in concrete approval procedures.

> «At issue is a decision on a construction project of the New Railway Link through the Alps (NEAT), which was issued by DETEC as the planning approval authority for major projects.»

**BVGE 2018 III/3** of 24 September 2018  
The Federal Administrative Court dealt with the heavy vehicle fee in connection with the financing of major railway projects.
The decision clarifies the connection between the flat-rate heavy vehicle fee and the financing of NEAT projects according to the original version of Art. 196 No. 3 BV.

> «Furthermore, the revenue from the HVF also serves the realisation of certain major railway projects (financing objective according to Art. 87a para. 2 lit. a BV [previously: Art. 196 No. 3 para. 2 lit. b and para. 5 BV] or Art. 19 para. 2 HVF Act).»

### Procedural Aspects

The case law shows that the transitional provisions of Art. 196 BV have not only substantive legal significance, but also have procedural consequences. This applies in particular to the time limitation of competences and the transitional obligations of the cantons. In doing so, the courts apply the general constitutional principles such as the principle of legality and the federal loyalty obligation.

The temporal limitation of various transitional regulations means that some provisions have lost their practical relevance (e.g., Art. 196 No. 2 para. 8 BV until the entry into force of the HVF Act), while others remain applicable (e.g., the time limitation of direct federal tax until the end of 2035).
