# Art. 197 FC — Transitional provisions

## Case law

The transitional provisions of the Federal Constitution govern the temporal application of new constitutional provisions and create legal certainty in the transition from old to new legal situations. The case law has dealt with various paragraphs of Art. 197 FC, with questions of the direct applicability of new constitutional provisions and their scope being particularly prominent.

### I. Para. 1 — Accession to the UN

There is no relevant constitutional case law on Art. 197 para. 1 FC (Switzerland's accession to the UN). Switzerland joined the UN on 10 September 2002; the transitional provision has since become moot.

### II. Para. 2 — Transitional provision to Art. 62 (Education)

**BGE 138 I 162** of 13 April 2012
Adequate special needs education for disabled children despite transitional provision.
The Federal Supreme Court dealt with the right to special needs education following the reform of fiscal equalisation.

> «In the field of special needs education, the cantons have considerable discretion. The minimum requirements under federal law only require an appropriate educational offering at public schools that is adequate based on experience, but not the optimal or most suitable education for a child.»

### III. Para. 3 — Transitional provision to Art. 83 (National roads)

There is no relevant constitutional case law on the transitional provision concerning national roads (para. 3). The provision primarily governs the cost allocation between the Confederation and the cantons for national road construction following the reform of fiscal equalisation.

### IV. Para. 4 — Transitional provision to Art. 112b (Promotion of integration of disabled persons)

There is no specific constitutional case law on the transitional provision concerning disability integration (para. 4). The provision governs the transfer of competence from disability insurance to the cantons.

### V. Para. 5 — Transitional provision to Art. 112c (Assistance for the elderly and disabled)

There is no relevant constitutional case law on the transitional provision concerning assistance for the elderly and disabled (para. 5). This provision has now been largely implemented.

### VI. Para. 6

This paragraph is repealed and without content.

### VII. Para. 7 — Transitional provision to Art. 120 (Genetic engineering in the non-human field)

There is no relevant constitutional case law on the transitional provision concerning the genetic engineering moratorium (para. 7). The five-year moratorium has expired.

### VIII. Para. 8 — Transitional provision to Art. 121 (Residence and permanent residence of foreign nationals)

**BGE 139 I 16** of 12 October 2012
Direct applicability of the deportation initiative denied.
The key decision on the transitional provision following the adoption of the deportation initiative.

> «Paragraphs 3-6 of Art. 121, incorporated into the Federal Constitution with the deportation initiative on 28 November 2010, are not directly applicable based on an interpretation committed to practical concordance and due to insufficient specificity, but require implementation by the legislature.»

### IX. Para. 9 — Transitional provision to Art. 75b (Second homes)

**BGE 139 II 243** of 22 May 2013
Direct applicability of the second home ban from 11 March 2012.
Fundamental decision on the immediate effect of the second home initiative.

> «Art. 75b para. 1 in connection with Art. 197 para. 9 para. 2 FC contains a directly applicable building permit ban for second homes in municipalities where the 20% share is reached or exceeded. This ban applies to all building permits that have been granted at first instance in the affected municipalities since 11 March 2012.»

**BGE 140 II 25** of 6 December 2013
Procedural aspects of the second home ban.
Confirmation of direct applicability in a zoning plan procedure.

> «The initiation of an official zoning plan procedure constitutes an independently contestable final decision if cantonal law provides that certain objections can only be raised by appeal against the initiation decision.»

**Judgment 1C_159/2012** of 14 December 2012
Application of the transitional provision in municipal planning procedures.
The Federal Supreme Court specified the scope of the immediate building permit ban.

**Judgment 1C_646/2012** of 22 May 2013
Retroactive effect on building permits issued before 1 January 2013.
Distinction between contestable and void building permits depending on the timing of issuance.

### X. Para. 10 — Transitional provision to Art. 95 para. 3

There is no relevant case law on the transitional provision concerning private economic activity of the Confederation (para. 10).

### XI. Para. 11 — Transitional provision to Art. 121a (Management of immigration)

There is no specific constitutional case law on the transitional provision of the mass immigration initiative (para. 11). The provision governed the implementation deadline for renegotiating international treaties, but was effectively superseded by the implementation of the initiative in the «light domestic preference» solution.

### XII. Para. 12 — Transitional provision to Art. 10a (Prohibition of covering one's face)

There is no constitutional case law yet on the transitional provision of the covering ban (para. 12), as the corresponding implementing legislation is still being developed. The transitional provision sets a deadline of two years for implementing legislation.

Provisional case law on cantonal covering bans:

**BGE 144 I 281** of 20 September 2018
Cantonal facial covering ban in Ticino assessed as constitutional.
The Federal Supreme Court examined the constitutional admissibility of a cantonal covering ban.

> «The Ticino laws on facial covering in public spaces provide for a conclusive list of exceptions to the facial covering ban. The ban thus formulated appears too broadly conceived from the perspective of freedom of expression.»

### XIII. Para. 13 — Transitional provision to Art. 117b (Nursing care)

There is no case law yet on the transitional provision concerning the nursing initiative (para. 13), as the provision was only adopted in 2021 and is in the implementation phase.

### XIV. Para. 14 — Transitional provision to Art. 118 para. 2 lit. b (Protection of health)

There is no case law yet on the transitional provision concerning tobacco products and electronic cigarettes (para. 14).

### XV. Para. 15 — Transitional provision to Art. 129a (Special taxation of large enterprise groups)

There is no case law yet on the transitional provision of the OECD minimum taxation (para. 15), as the provision was only adopted in 2023 and is in implementation.

**Judgment 1C_565/2024** of 18 February 2025
Cantonal vote on tax law amendment in connection with minimum taxation.
First procedural aspects of implementation at cantonal level.

### XVI. Para. 16 — Transitional provision to Art. 112 (13th AHV pension)

There is no specific case law yet on the transitional provision of the 13th AHV pension (para. 16), as the provision was only adopted in 2024. Implementation takes place gradually from the second calendar year after adoption.

General case law on AHV pension calculation:

**BGE 135 III 20** of 4 September 2008
Non-seizability of cantonal supplementary benefits to the AHV.
Fundamental considerations on the protection of AHV benefits.

**BGE 144 III 407** of 6 June 2018
Absolute non-seizability of AHV pensions confirmed.
The Federal Supreme Court reaffirmed the comprehensive protection of AHV pensions from seizure.
