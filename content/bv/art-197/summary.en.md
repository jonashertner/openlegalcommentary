# Art. 197 Federal Constitution — Transitional Provisions

## Overview

Art. 197 Federal Constitution contains the transitional provisions to the Federal Constitution. This provision regulates how new constitutional norms enter into force temporally. It resolves legal conflicts between old and new constitutional law. Art. 197 Federal Constitution does not belong to substantive constitutional law, but is intertemporal law (temporal transitional law).

The provision grows with every constitutional amendment. When the people and cantons accept a constitutional amendment, a new paragraph is added. Today Art. 197 Federal Constitution comprises sixteen paragraphs. They range from UN membership (paragraph 1) to the 13th AHV pension (paragraph 16).

Transitional provisions fulfil three important functions: They create legal certainty during the transition from old to new legal situation. They give the legislature deadlines for the implementation of new constitutional provisions. They regulate whether a constitutional provision is immediately applicable or only after the enactment of implementing legislation.

Case law distinguishes between directly applicable and non-directly applicable transitional provisions. Only sufficiently determined norms that do not contradict superior law are directly applicable. The Federal Supreme Court affirmed the direct applicability of the second homes initiative (**BGE 139 II 243**). However, it denied it for the deportation initiative due to conflicts with international law (**BGE 139 I 16**).

**Concrete examples**: The second homes initiative (paragraph 9) immediately prohibited new second homes in municipalities with over 20 percent second home share from 11 March 2012. Granted building permits became void. The care initiative (paragraph 13) gives the Federal Council 18 months for immediate measures against the shortage of nursing professionals. The legislature has four years for the definitive implementation.

For citizens, transitional provisions are relevant when new constitutional rights apply immediately. For authorities, they regulate deadlines for action and subsidiary competences. When deadlines expire, the Federal Council can enact ordinances if the legislature is in default.
