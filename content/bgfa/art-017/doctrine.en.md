# Art. 17 BGFA – Disciplinary Measures

## Doctrine

### 1. Legislative History

**N. 1** Art. 17 BGFA originates from the Federal Council's draft Message of 28 April 1999 (BBl 1999 6013). The draft already provided for the graduated catalogue of measures that the statute contains today. The Federal Council justified the exhaustive regulation of disciplinary law at federal level on the ground that intercantonal freedom of movement for lawyers — the declared primary purpose of the BGFA — presupposes harmonisation of sanction mechanisms (BBl 1999 6057 f.). Divergent cantonal disciplinary regimes would have been incompatible with the uniformity of professional law that was sought.

**N. 2** Compared to the previously purely cantonal law, Art. 17 BGFA represents a significant innovation: until the BGFA entered into force on 1 June 2002, disciplinary authority rested exclusively with the cantons, which had different catalogues of measures, some of which were based on the professional rules of cantonal bar associations. The parliamentary deliberations gave rise to no substantive controversy over the catalogue of measures itself; the only disputed matters were the relationship to cantonal law and the question of transitional arrangements (**BGE 130 II 270** E. 1).

**N. 3** With the graduated catalogue (reprimand through to permanent ban on practising), the Federal Council sought to create a framework that ensures proportionality through statutory structure. The possibility of imposing a fine in addition to a ban on practising (Art. 17 para. 2 BGFA) was already contained in the draft Message (BBl 1999 6059). The provisional ban on practising under Art. 17 para. 3 BGFA completes the sanctions framework with an interim protective measure.

---

### 2. Systematic Classification

**N. 4** Art. 17 BGFA is situated in the third section of the BGFA («Supervision», Arts. 14–20) and forms the centrepiece of disciplinary law governing the legal profession. The provision defines the legal consequences for violations of the BGFA, while Arts. 14–16 BGFA govern the supervisory authority and the procedure for disciplinary investigations, and Arts. 18–20 BGFA regulate the notification and deletion of disciplinary measures from the register.

**N. 5** The BGFA governs disciplinary law exhaustively (**BGE 130 II 270** E. 1.1; **BGE 129 II 297** E. 1.1; **BGE 132 II 250** E. 4.3.1; **BGE 150 II 308** E. 7.4). There is therefore no basis in federal law for cantonal disciplinary measures that go beyond the catalogue of Art. 17 BGFA. In particular, ordering the publication of disciplinary measures in official cantonal gazettes is contrary to federal law, since this constitutes an independent repressive sanction that is not provided for in the catalogue of Art. 17 BGFA (**BGE 150 II 308** E. 7.9). This follows from the primacy of federal law under Art. 49 para. 1 FC.

**N. 6** ↔ Art. 12 BGFA: Art. 17 BGFA enforces the professional duties contained in Art. 12 BGFA (and the BGFA as a whole). Any violation of «this Act» — not only of the professional duties under Art. 12, but also of the other requirements (Arts. 7 ff.) — may trigger a disciplinary measure. → Art. 9 BGFA: Deletion from the register of lawyers under Art. 9 BGFA is an administrative measure and must be strictly distinguished from the disciplinary measure under Art. 17 BGFA (see N. 12). → Art. 18 BGFA: Disciplinary measures must be entered in the register and communicated to the competent authorities. → Art. 20 BGFA: The time limits for deletion of disciplinary measures from the register.

### 3. Content of the Provision

#### 3.1 Prerequisites: Violation of the Act

**N. 7** The prerequisite for ordering a disciplinary measure is a violation of the BGFA («In the event of a violation of this Act», Art. 17 para. 1 BGFA). The concept is to be understood broadly: it covers breaches of the professional rules (Art. 12 BGFA), the professional secret (Art. 13 BGFA), and breaches of the conditions for registration. The Federal Supreme Court reviews the existence of conduct warranting disciplinary action with full cognition (judgments 2C_980/2016 of 7.3.2017 E. 3.2; 2C_536/2018 of 25.2.2019 E. 4.1).

**N. 8** Not every breach of professional duties justifies disciplinary action. According to established case law, state intervention requires that the negligent exercise of the profession objectively reaches a degree of seriousness such that an additional sanction is in the overriding public interest and appears proportionate. A *qualified* or *significant* breach of professional duties is required («un manquement significatif aux devoirs de la profession»; **BGE 144 II 473** E. 4.1; judgment 2C_250/2021 of 3.11.2021 E. 5.1).

#### 3.2 The Catalogue of Measures (Art. 17 para. 1 BGFA)

**N. 9** The catalogue in Art. 17 para. 1 BGFA is exhaustive (Poledna, BSK BGFA, Art. 17 N. 1; Bauer/Bauer, CR LLCA, Art. 17 N. 1). It provides for five measures in ascending order of severity:

- **lit. a: reprimand** — the mildest measure; appropriate for minor violations that do not require a particular preventive effect;
- **lit. b: censure** — more severe than the reprimand; appropriate for more significant but not serious violations;
- **lit. c: fine of up to CHF 20,000** — appropriate for intermediate violations; may be imposed in addition to a ban on practising pursuant to Art. 17 para. 2 BGFA;
- **lit. d: temporary ban on practising of up to two years** — the most incisive time-limited measure; generally applicable only in cases of repetition or particularly grave first-time violations (judgment 2A.177/2005 of 24.2.2006 E. 4.1; judgment 2C_980/2016 of 7.3.2017 E. 3.2; judgment 2C_536/2018 of 25.2.2019 E. 4.2);
- **lit. e: permanent ban on practising** — the most severe measure; as a rule requires aggravating circumstances.

**N. 10** The catalogue is to be understood as a graduated scale that must be applied in accordance with the principle of proportionality (Art. 5 para. 2 FC). Less severe measures are to be preferred in principle, provided they are capable of deterring the person concerned from further misconduct and of protecting the public (**BGE 150 II 308** E. 7.6).

**N. 11** The *temporary ban on practising* (Art. 17 para. 1 lit. d BGFA) is the most severe time-limited measure. It is permissible in cases of repetition or exceptionally grave first-time violations (judgment 2C_980/2016 E. 3.2; judgment 2C_536/2018 E. 4.2). When reviewing proportionality in the determination of the measure, the Federal Supreme Court places great weight on: the gravity of the violation (serious/moderate/minor), the degree of fault, the professional background (prior disciplinary sanctions, see N. 17–18), and the need for deterrence. The ban on practising prohibits forensic activity as a lawyer; advisory activity without appearing in the reserved domain remains permissible, however (BBl 1999 6060; Fellmann, Anwaltsrecht, 2nd ed. 2017, para. 740).

#### 3.3 Cumulation of Fine and Ban on Practising (Art. 17 para. 2 BGFA)

**N. 12** Art. 17 para. 2 BGFA permits the imposition of a fine in addition to a ban on practising. Cumulation is expressly limited to this combination; a fine may not be cumulated with a reprimand or a censure. This reflects the legislative decision to permit a double burden only in serious cases.

#### 3.4 Provisional Ban on Practising (Art. 17 para. 3 BGFA)

**N. 13** The provisional ban on practising is a protective measure, not a sanction in the legal sense. Its ordering requires that it be «necessary», which presupposes an urgent need to protect the public. It is subject to the general conditions applicable to provisional measures: urgency, a risk that the protective purpose would be jeopardised by delay, and proportionality.

### 4. Legal Consequences

#### 4.1 Exhaustive Character of the Catalogue of Measures

**N. 14** The exhaustive character of Art. 17 BGFA means that the supervisory authority may not impose any measures other than those provided for in the catalogue — whether more severe or more lenient (Poledna, BSK BGFA, Art. 17 N. 1; Bauer/Bauer, CR LLCA, Art. 17 N. 1; **BGE 150 II 308** E. 7.4). Neither publication in the Official Gazette nor a reprimand with conditions not provided for by statute may be ordered.

#### 4.2 Disciplinary Measure versus Administrative Measure

**N. 15** Art. 17 BGFA (disciplinary measure) and Art. 9 BGFA (deletion from the register as an administrative measure) must be distinguished from each other. Both procedures are independent of each other; deletion from the register does not preclude a parallel or subsequent disciplinary investigation, and a temporary ban on practising does not result in deletion if the lawyer continues to satisfy the personal conditions for registration under Arts. 7–8 BGFA (**BGE 137 II 425** E. 7.2; Bauer/Bauer, CR LLCA, Art. 17 N. 20; Bohnet/Martenet, Droit de la profession d'avocat, 2009, N. 2239).

#### 4.3 Cognition of the Federal Supreme Court

**N. 16** The Federal Supreme Court reviews the *question of whether conduct warranting disciplinary action exists* with full cognition. It exercises restraint, however, with regard to the determination of the specific measure: it intervenes only if the sanction exceeds the bounds of proper discretion and appears clearly disproportionate or indeed arbitrary (judgments 2C_980/2016 E. 3.2; 2C_536/2018 E. 4.1; 2C_250/2021 E. 5.2).

#### 4.4 Taking Prior Disciplinary Measures into Account in the Determination of Sanctions

**N. 17** The supervisory authority must determine the disciplinary measure taking into account the person's professional background. According to **BGE 150 II 308** E. 5.10, prior disciplinary sanctions — including measures already deleted from the cantonal register of lawyers — may be taken into account in the determination of sanctions. The prohibition on use in criminal proceedings under repealed former Art. 369 para. 7 SCC does not preclude this. An analogous prohibition on use does not follow from the register law framework of the BGFA (Art. 20 BGFA) either, since that framework only governs the obligation to maintain the register and does not establish any restriction on consideration (**BGE 150 II 308** E. 5.7).

**N. 18** However, with the passage of time, the significance of past disciplinary measures diminishes. The longer a person has conducted themselves impeccably, the less a previous transgression may be held against them in the context of new proceedings (**BGE 150 II 308** E. 5.8). Brunner/Henn/Kriesi (Anwaltsrecht, 2015, p. 251), by contrast, take the view that measures deleted from the register must not be taken into account; the Federal Supreme Court expressly rejected this position in **BGE 150 II 308**.

#### 4.5 Effects on Register Law

**N. 19** Disciplinary measures that have been ordered must be entered in the cantonal register of lawyers (Art. 5 para. 2 lit. e BGFA) and communicated to the supervisory authorities of all cantons (Art. 18 para. 2 BGFA). The time limits for deletion are governed by Art. 20 BGFA: reprimands, censures, and fines are deleted after five years; temporary bans on practising are deleted after ten years from the date of lifting.

### 5. Disputed Questions

#### 5.1 Delimitation of Disciplinary Law from Cantonal Law

**N. 20** It was initially disputed whether the BGFA governs disciplinary law exhaustively or whether the cantons may provide for supplementary measures. The Federal Supreme Court resolved this question at an early stage and consistently in favour of exhaustiveness (**BGE 129 II 297** E. 1.1; **BGE 130 II 270** E. 1.1; **BGE 132 II 250** E. 4.3.1). Poledna (BSK BGFA, Art. 14 N. 9) takes the view that the cantons may provide for *supervisory* means going beyond the BGFA (but not disciplinary measures), a position that case law has in principle confirmed. The question of publication of disciplinary measures in the Official Gazette has been resolved as contrary to federal law by **BGE 150 II 308** E. 7.9.

#### 5.2 Threshold for Disciplinary Relevance

**N. 21** It is disputed from what degree of seriousness a breach of duty becomes disciplinarily relevant. The Federal Supreme Court requires a «significant violation» (**BGE 144 II 473** E. 4.1). Fellmann (Anwaltsrecht, 2nd ed. 2017, para. 728 ff.) and Bohnet/Martenet (Droit de la profession d'avocat, 2009, N. 688 ff.) emphasise that the disciplinary authorities must observe a qualification threshold in order to prevent an excessive extension of disciplinary authority. In practice, this threshold is assessed on a case-by-case basis; no fixed limit exists.

#### 5.3 Taking Deleted Disciplinary Measures into Account

**N. 22** The most significant current disputed question concerns the taking into account of disciplinary measures deleted from the register in new proceedings (N. 17–18). Brunner/Henn/Kriesi (Anwaltsrecht, 2015, p. 251) reject this on the ground that the time limits for deletion under Art. 20 BGFA are intended to protect a rehabilitation interest comparable to that under criminal law. The Federal Supreme Court in **BGE 150 II 308** E. 5.10 expressly rejected this view and approved the taking into account of such measures — subject to the proviso of diminishing weight with the passage of time.

#### 5.4 Ban on Practising for a First-Time Violation

**N. 23** It is disputed whether a temporary ban on practising may be ordered already for a first-time violation. Case law answers this in the affirmative in the case of «serious» first-time violations (judgment 2A.177/2005 of 24.2.2006 E. 4.1; judgment 2C_980/2016 E. 3.2). Fellmann (Anwaltsrecht, 2nd ed. 2017, para. 735) and Bohnet/Martenet (Droit de la profession d'avocat, 2009, N. 699) fundamentally agree, but require a stricter proportionality review than in cases of repetition. The boundary between «moderately serious» and «serious» is established by the Federal Supreme Court on a case-by-case basis, with it exercising considerable restraint (judgment 2C_536/2018 E. 5.2–5.3).

### 6. Practical Notes

**N. 24** **Relationship between disciplinary and administrative proceedings:** Lawyers and supervisory authorities must carefully distinguish between the disciplinary procedure under Arts. 14–17 BGFA and the administrative procedure for register management (Arts. 6–9 BGFA). Both procedures may be conducted in parallel; a criminal conviction may lead both to deletion under Art. 9 BGFA and — though not necessarily — to a disciplinary measure under Art. 17 BGFA (**BGE 137 II 425** E. 7.2).

**N. 25** **Procedural law:** The BGFA does not govern procedure exhaustively; the cantons have jurisdiction (Art. 34 para. 1 BGFA). A judicial authority must decide as the last cantonal instance. The Federal Supreme Court reviews disciplinary decisions in matters of professional law under the procedure of appeal in public law matters (Art. 82 lit. a BGG). Art. 6 para. 1 ECHR is applicable to disciplinary proceedings before judicial authorities, since a temporary ban on practising constitutes a civil law dispute within the meaning of the Convention (judgment 2C_980/2016 E. 2.1.1 with reference to **BGE 131 I 467** E. 2.5).

**N. 26** **Statement by the registration authority:** If a supervisory authority intends to order a disciplinary measure against a lawyer not registered in its own canton, it must give the supervisory authority of the canton of registration the opportunity to submit a statement (Art. 16 para. 2 BGFA). This allows the supervisory authority of the canton of registration to become aware of violations, which it may in turn take into account in future determinations of sanctions (**BGE 150 II 308** E. 5.9).

**N. 27** **Publication:** A cantonal order to publish a disciplinary measure in the Official Gazette is contrary to federal law by virtue of the exhaustive regulation in Art. 17 BGFA (**BGE 150 II 308** E. 7.9). Supervisory authorities may not order such a measure, even if cantonal law provides a basis for doing so.

**N. 28** **Determination of sanctions:** In selecting the measure, the following must be taken into account: the nature and gravity of the violation, the degree of fault, prior disciplinary sanctions (including deleted ones, with diminishing weight), the insight shown by the person concerned, and the need for deterrence. The Federal Supreme Court intervenes only in cases of clear disproportionality. Practice shows that reprimands and censures are used for minor to moderately serious first-time transgressions; fines for intermediate cases; and bans on practising for serious or repeated violations.

**N. 29** **Advisory activity despite a ban on practising:** The temporary ban on practising under Art. 17 para. 1 lit. d BGFA prohibits only forensic activity in the reserved domain. The sanctioned person remains free to continue providing legal advice, provided they do not present themselves as a «lawyer» («Rechtsanwältin» or «Rechtsanwalt») in the reserved domain (BBl 1999 6060; Fellmann, Anwaltsrecht, 2nd ed. 2017, para. 740; **BGE 150 II 308** E. 7.8).
