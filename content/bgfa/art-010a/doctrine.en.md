# Art. 10a BGFA — Notification

## Doctrine

### 1. Legislative History

**N. 1** Art. 10a BGFA is not an original element of the Lawyers Act of 23 June 2000 (AS 2002 863). The provision was inserted into the BGFA by the Federal Act of 18 June 2010 on the Enterprise Identification Number (UIDG, SR 431.03) and entered into force simultaneously with the UIDG on 1 January 2011 (AS 2010 4667, 4679; Annex to the UIDG, item II.15). The original Federal Council Message on the Lawyers Act of 28 April 1999 (BBl 1999 6013) contained no counterpart to this provision, as the UID system did not yet exist at that time.

**N. 2** The Federal Council Message on the UIDG of 27 August 2008 (BBl 2008 7745) sets out the objectives of the cross-sectoral UID system: the aim is to introduce a uniform, Switzerland-wide unique identification number for enterprises and other entities in order to simplify and standardise the exchange of data between authorities (BBl 2008 7749 ff.). In order for law firms and the legal practitioners associated with them to be captured as UID entities, amendments to a large number of register statutes — including the BGFA — were required. The Federal Council described these amendments as technical-administrative consequential changes (BBl 2008 7807).

**N. 3** The parliamentary deliberations on the UIDG proceeded without any material amendment to the provisions on register adjustments. The connection of the cantonal registers of lawyers to the UID system was uncontested during the parliamentary procedure (cf. AB 2009 NR 1921; AB 2010 SR 248). Art. 10a BGFA thus represents a technical-functional extension of the register law part of the BGFA, not a substantive change to the law governing the legal profession.

---

### 2. Systematic Classification

**N. 4** Art. 10a BGFA is located in the first chapter of the BGFA (Arts. 1–11: «Freedom of movement and registers») and integrates into the register-law systematics of Arts. 5–10 BGFA. While Art. 5 BGFA determines the content of the cantonal register of lawyers and Art. 10 BGFA governs access to the register, Art. 10a BGFA creates a specific duty to notify the Federal Statistical Office (FSO) for a specifically defined statutory purpose. ↔ Art. 5 BGFA (content of the register); ↔ Art. 10 BGFA (right of access to the register).

**N. 5** The provision constitutes a sector-specific special rule in relation to the general exchange of information between authorities as provided for in the UIDG. Art. 10a BGFA is to be read as lex specialis in relation to Art. 13 UIDG: Art. 13 UIDG governs the general obligation of UID offices to report data to the FSO; Art. 10a BGFA gives concrete form to that obligation for the BGFA register by expressly designating the cantonal register-keeping authorities (→ Art. 14 BGFA) as the notifying entities. → Art. 13 UIDG (general reporting obligation of UID offices); → Art. 4 UIDG (assignment of the UID by the FSO).

**N. 6** The provision touches upon the relationship between the federally regulated register of lawyers and the likewise federal UID system. Both are governed by federal law; however, responsibility for keeping the register lies with the cantons pursuant to Art. 14 BGFA. Art. 10a BGFA thus establishes a federal-law obligation on cantonal authorities to transmit data to a federal authority. → Art. 14 BGFA (jurisdiction of the cantonal supervisory authorities).

**N. 7** Art. 10a BGFA differs from Art. 10 BGFA in one essential respect: Art. 10 BGFA governs the right of access — i.e. access granted on the initiative of the entitled party — whereas Art. 10a BGFA establishes an active notification obligation originating with the register-keeping authority. Data transmission does not occur upon request but ex officio, as soon and insofar as the UID-relevant particulars are present in the register. Bohnet/Martenet, Droit de la profession d'avocat, 2009, pp. 316 f., emphasise in relation to Art. 10 BGFA the contrast between the authorities' right of access and public disclosure; in the case of Art. 10a BGFA, both these figures are replaced by the statutory notification obligation ex lege.

### 3. Content of the Provision

#### 3.1 Subject Matter of the Notification: «data required for the assignment and use of the enterprise identification number»

**N. 8** Art. 10a BGFA defines the subject matter of the notification by means of a purpose limitation: the data from the register that must and may be communicated are those that are «required for the assignment and use of the enterprise identification number pursuant to the Federal Act of 18 June 2010 on the Enterprise Identification Number». The scope of data is therefore not exhaustively enumerated in the BGFA itself, but follows from the UIDG and the Ordinance thereon (UIDV, SR 431.031).

**N. 9** Pursuant to Art. 6 UIDG in conjunction with Art. 5 UIDV, the characteristics of an entity relevant for the assignment of a UID include in particular: name or designation, legal form, registered office, address and — for natural persons as UID entities — date of birth and place of origin. From the BGFA register (Art. 5 para. 2 BGFA), these are principally the particulars necessary for the identification of the lawyer as a practising professional, in particular surname, given name, office address and — where applicable — business address. The reference to «required» data constitutes a data protection limitation that excludes the transmission of all register contents (e.g. disciplinary measures, → Art. 5 para. 2 lit. e BGFA).

**N. 10** The UIDG recognises different categories of UID entities (Art. 3 UIDG). In the context of the BGFA, the following are relevant in particular: (a) natural persons practising as sole practitioners; (b) enterprises in the legal form of a corporation (AG, GmbH) or a general partnership operating a law firm; and (c) other entities within the meaning of Art. 3 para. 1 lit. c UIDG. The decisive point is that it is not the register entry as such but the underlying practitioner who is captured as a UID entity. Staehelin/Oetiker, in: Fellmann/Zindel (eds.), BSK Anwaltsgesetz, 2nd ed. 2019, Art. 10a N. 1, characterise the UID-relevant data contained in the BGFA register as a subset of the register contents pursuant to Art. 5 para. 2 BGFA, which requires an independent, purpose-bound legal basis for transmission — namely Art. 10a BGFA.

#### 3.2 Receiving Authority: Federal Statistical Office

**N. 11** The notification is made exclusively to the Federal Statistical Office (FSO), which pursuant to Art. 4 para. 1 UIDG is the sole authority responsible for the assignment of UID numbers. The FSO maintains the UID register and is responsible for the management and quality assurance of UID data (cf. Judgment BVGer A-1519/2021 of 21 January 2022, consid. 1.1 and Facts A–E on the functioning of the UID register). Any onward transmission of the data by the FSO to third parties is governed by the UIDG, in particular Arts. 13 f. UIDG, and not by the BGFA.

**N. 12** The entities subject to the notification obligation are, in accordance with the purpose of the provision, the cantonal supervisory authorities that keep the register (→ Art. 14 BGFA). The BGFA does not expressly designate the obligated entities, but it follows from Art. 5 BGFA (keeping of the register by cantonal authorities) and the context of the provision that only the authority keeping the register is both obligated and entitled to make the notification. The cantonal authorities act in that capacity as UID offices within the meaning of Art. 3 para. 1 lit. a UIDG in conjunction with Art. 3 UIDV. Fellmann, Anwaltsrecht, 2nd ed. 2017, para. 366, notes that the register-keeping authorities pursuant to Art. 10a BGFA belong to the circle of UID offices subject to the reporting obligation within the meaning of the UIDG.

#### 3.3 Modality: Notification Ex Officio

**N. 13** The wording «shall be notified» employs the passive voice and thereby expresses an obligation, not merely a power. The notification does not occur upon application by the FSO but ex officio, as soon as the relevant data are present in the register or have been modified. This corresponds to the fundamental principle of the UID system, which is based on the continuous maintenance of register data (cf. Art. 10 UIDG: ongoing updating obligation of UID offices). The temporal modality (frequency, intervals) of the notification is governed by Art. 10 UIDG and the UIDV; Art. 10a BGFA contains no independent rule in this regard.

#### 3.4 Data Protection Classification

**N. 14** Art. 10a BGFA establishes a legal basis for the processing and transmission of personal data within the meaning of Art. 31 FADP (SR 235.1). Without this legal basis, the disclosure of register-of-lawyers data to the FSO — insofar as it concerns data relating to natural persons — would require the express consent of the persons concerned or would only be permissible in the context of overriding public interests. Art. 10a BGFA creates this legal basis within the meaning of Art. 31 para. 2 lit. b FADP. The transmitted data are to be further processed by the FSO in accordance with the rules of the UIDG; in particular, the purpose limitation under Art. 11 UIDG applies. Staehelin/Oetiker, BSK Anwaltsgesetz, 2nd ed. 2019, Art. 10a N. 2, emphasise that Art. 10a BGFA, as a formal statutory basis, satisfies the requirements of Art. 36 para. 1 FC for the restriction of the fundamental right to informational self-determination (Art. 13 para. 2 FC) associated with the transmission of data.

---

### 4. Legal Consequences

**N. 15** Art. 10a BGFA establishes a **legal obligation** on the part of the cantonal register-keeping authorities to transmit data. The obligation is — unlike the authorities' right of access under Art. 10 para. 1 BGFA — not dependent on an application and is also not subject to the discretion of the authority; it arises ex lege upon the existence of UID-relevant data in the register.

**N. 16** The lawyers concerned have no right against the register-keeping authority to prevent the transmission from taking place. The statutory basis in Art. 10a BGFA in conjunction with Art. 13 UIDG justifies the data transmission irrespective of the wishes of the registered person. However, the persons concerned retain the data protection rights of access to and rectification of data vis-à-vis the FSO as data controller (Arts. 25 f. FADP; Arts. 16 f. UIDG).

**N. 17** A breach of the notification obligation by the cantonal authority does not give rise to direct sanctions under the BGFA; however, it may be relevant from a supervisory law perspective and — to the extent that failure to report leads to an incorrect UID assignment or to problems in official data exchange — may entail administrative law consequences. The UIDG does not provide for specific sanctions against UID offices that fail to comply with their reporting obligations; the general federal oversight mechanisms in relation to the cantons remain reserved.

**N. 18** The legal effect on the recipient side: upon receipt of the notification, the FSO assigns a UID to the UID entity (lawyer, law firm) or updates the existing entry (Art. 4 para. 1 UIDG). The UID then serves as the unique identifier of that entity throughout Swiss administrative dealings (Art. 1 UIDG). For practitioners, the UID is of practical significance notably in value added tax law (Art. 29a MWSTV), in the commercial register, and when issuing invoices to public law clients.

### 5. Contested Issues

**N. 19** **Scope of data to be transmitted.** In the literature, the question of which fields of the BGFA register specifically fall within «the data required for the assignment and use of the UID» has not been conclusively resolved. Staehelin/Oetiker, BSK Anwaltsgesetz, 2nd ed. 2019, Art. 10a N. 1, take the view that only the identification particulars of the person (name, address, legal form of any law firm) may be communicated, but not register-specific professional law data such as disciplinary measures or the conditions for registration. This position corresponds to the wording of the statute («required») and the purpose limitation principle of the UIDG (Art. 11 UIDG). No contrary view advocating a broader scope of data has been advanced in the literature; however, the question of the precise delimitation has not been decided by a superior court.

**N. 20** **Relationship to the data protection consent requirement.** The question of whether Art. 10a BGFA as a formal statutory basis is sufficient or whether the express consent of the registered persons is required was not discussed controversially in the legal literature. Fellmann, Anwaltsrecht, 2nd ed. 2017, para. 366, and Staehelin/Oetiker, BSK Anwaltsgesetz, 2nd ed. 2019, Art. 10a N. 2, agree that Art. 10a BGFA suffices as a formal statutory basis, since the transmission serves a clearly defined public purpose and is limited to what is necessary for that purpose. The new Federal Act on Data Protection (FADP 2020, in force since 1 September 2023) confirms this outcome: pursuant to Art. 31 para. 2 lit. b FADP, processing without consent is permissible where it rests on a statutory basis. Art. 10a BGFA satisfies this requirement.

**N. 21** **Cantonal implementing provisions.** Art. 10a BGFA regulates the notification obligation at the federal level. It is unresolved whether the cantons may enact supplementary implementing provisions (e.g. concerning the timing, format, and procedure for data transmission) or whether these matters are conclusively regulated by the UIDG and the UIDV. Bohnet/Martenet, Droit de la profession d'avocat, 2009, pp. 296 ff., emphasise with respect to the register law part of the BGFA generally that cantonal implementing provisions may only give concrete form to the federal regulation, but may neither restrict nor extend it. These principles apply mutatis mutandis to Art. 10a BGFA: procedural implementing provisions by the cantons are permissible insofar as they are compatible with Art. 10a BGFA and the UIDG.

### 6. Practical Notes

**N. 22** **For cantonal register-keeping authorities:** Art. 10a BGFA obliges the cantonal supervisory authority (→ Art. 14 BGFA) to report UID-relevant data from the register of lawyers proactively and on an ongoing basis to the FSO. In practice, this is typically done via automated data interfaces between the cantonal register systems and the FSO's UID register. Upon every registration, modification, or deletion in the register of lawyers (→ Arts. 6, 9 BGFA), the modified UID-relevant particulars must be forwarded without delay. If notification is omitted, the law firm or lawyer risks having no UID or an incorrect one, which may give rise to administrative problems in dealings with other authorities.

**N. 23** **For lawyers:** Persons registered in the register of lawyers must expect that their identification particulars will be transmitted to the FSO pursuant to Art. 10a BGFA and published in the UID register. Changes (e.g. change of address, change of law firm) that are notified to the register of lawyers will also enter the UID register. Lawyers who require their UID number for invoicing or official correspondence may query it through the FSO (UID register, www.uid.admin.ch). Requests for rectification of UID data — to the extent that such data originate from the BGFA register — should be directed in the first instance to the cantonal supervisory authority that keeps the source register.

**N. 24** **Data protection compliance:** Since Art. 10a BGFA limits data transmission to the FSO to what is «required», the cantons must ensure that their interfaces and data transmission processes do not transfer any data going beyond the UID purpose. In particular, information on disciplinary measures (→ Art. 5 para. 2 lit. e BGFA), bans on practising (→ Art. 17 para. 1 lit. e BGFA), or internal procedural register data must not be transmitted under Art. 10a BGFA. A data protection impact assessment pursuant to Art. 22 FADP is advisable where the data transmission takes place systematically and on a large scale.

**N. 25** **Relationship to Art. 10 BGFA in practice:** Art. 10a BGFA and Art. 10 BGFA do not exclude each other. The UID data transmitted pursuant to Art. 10a BGFA are available to the FSO for the administration of the UID register; the entities entitled under Art. 10 BGFA (authorities, supervisory authorities of other cantons) may continue to — on the basis of Art. 10 BGFA — request access to the BGFA register. Both provisions pursue different purposes and are to be applied in parallel.
