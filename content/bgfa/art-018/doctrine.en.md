# Art. 18 BGFA — Scope of Application of the Prohibition on Practice

## Doctrine

### 1. Legislative History

**N. 1** Art. 18 BGFA forms part of the section on disciplinary measures (Art. 14–20 BGFA) and was introduced by the Federal Act of 23 June 2000 on Freedom of Movement for Lawyers (BGFA, SR 935.61), which entered into force on 1 June 2002. In the Federal Council's Dispatch of 28 April 1999, the legislature noted that a temporary or permanent prohibition on practice constitutes the most severe disciplinary measure, since it prevents lawyers from representing parties before judicial authorities within the scope of the lawyers' monopoly; however, they may continue to engage in other activities — in particular, legal advice (BBl 1999 6013, 6060). This statement was uncontested in the parliamentary debates (AB N 1551 ff., 1568; AB S 1158 ff., 1173).

**N. 2** The legislature's primary purpose in enacting Art. 18 para. 1 BGFA was to ensure the intercantonal effect of the prohibition on practice: under the prior legal regime, enforcement was limited to the canton concerned. The nationwide scope of application is a direct consequence of the intercantonal freedom of movement created by the BGFA; it would be contrary to the system if a lawyer subject to a prohibition on practice in one canton could continue that activity in another canton (BBl 1999 6013, 6059 f.; BGE 150 II 308 E. 7.8). Art. 18 para. 2 BGFA ensures practical enforcement by imposing a duty to notify all cantonal supervisory authorities.

---

### 2. Systematic Classification

**N. 3** Art. 18 BGFA is located in the second section of the third chapter of the BGFA («Professional rules and disciplinary law», Art. 12–20 BGFA) and governs exclusively the territorial dimension of the most severe disciplinary measure provided for in the BGFA. The provision is not an independent legal basis for imposing a prohibition on practice but is an ancillary rule to → Art. 17 para. 1 lit. d and e BGFA, which provide for temporary (maximum two years) and permanent prohibitions on practice as disciplinary measures.

**N. 4** The intercantonal dimension of Art. 18 para. 1 BGFA corresponds to the intercantonal freedom of movement established by → Art. 4 BGFA (conditions for registration) and → Art. 6 para. 1 BGFA (effect of registration): registration entitles a lawyer to practise within the scope of the lawyers' monopoly before the authorities of all cantons, while a prohibition on practice equally withdraws that entitlement in all cantons. The prohibition and the freedom thus stand as mirror images of each other (↔ Art. 4, Art. 6 BGFA).

**N. 5** Art. 18 para. 2 BGFA is materially connected to → Art. 10 para. 1 BGFA (access to the register by judicial and administrative authorities and other authorised persons), → Art. 5 para. 2 lit. e BGFA (content of the cantonal register, in particular undeleted disciplinary measures), and → Art. 16 para. 2 BGFA (coordination between supervisory authorities in disciplinary proceedings). The BGFA exhaustively regulates disciplinary law in relation to lawyers; cantons may not provide for other, more lenient, or more severe measures (**BGE 150 II 308 E. 7.4**; **BGE 132 II 250 E. 4.3.1**; **BGE 130 II 270 E. 1.1**).

---

### 3. Elements of the Provision / Normative Content

#### 3.1 Paragraph 1: Nationwide Scope of Application

**N. 6** Art. 18 para. 1 BGFA provides that a prohibition on practice — whether temporary (Art. 17 para. 1 lit. d BGFA) or permanent (Art. 17 para. 1 lit. e BGFA) — applies throughout the **entire territory of Switzerland**. This rule is mandatory and does not permit any territorial limitation to individual cantons or parts of the national territory. Bauer/Bauer, CR Loi sur les avocats, 2nd ed. 2022, Art. 18 N. 1 confirm that Art. 18 para. 1 BGFA exhaustively governs the territorial scope of the prohibition on practice at the federal level.

**N. 7** «Prohibition on practice» within the meaning of Art. 18 BGFA refers to the prohibition of legal activity within the scope of the **lawyers' monopoly**, i.e. professional representation of parties before judicial authorities (→ Art. 2 para. 1 BGFA). Pure legal advice outside the scope of the monopoly is not covered; accordingly, a sanctioned lawyer may continue to provide advisory services during a prohibition on practice (BBl 1999 6013, 6060; **BGE 150 II 308 E. 7.8**; Poledna, in: Fellmann/Zindel [eds.], Kommentar zum Anwaltsgesetz, 2nd ed. 2011, Art. 17 N. 37). During the period of the prohibition, the person concerned may furthermore not refer to their entry in the register (→ Art. 11 para. 2 BGFA; cf. § 25 para. 2 KAG/BE).

**N. 8** The register entry subsists during a temporary prohibition on practice but is supplemented by a corresponding annotation (→ Art. 5 para. 2 lit. e BGFA; Art. 10 para. 2 BGFA). Federal and cantonal judicial and administrative authorities before which lawyers appear have access to this register entry (→ Art. 10 para. 1 BGFA). The prohibition on practice can thus be enforced across all authorities without the need for a separate official publication.

#### 3.2 Paragraph 2: Duty to Notify

**N. 9** Art. 18 para. 2 BGFA imposes a **duty to notify** on the imposing supervisory authority vis-à-vis the supervisory authorities of **all other cantons**. This duty is a technical enforcement measure intended to ensure that all cantonal supervisory bodies are immediately informed of the imposition of the prohibition. Notification must be effected upon the prohibition becoming enforceable; cantonal implementing legislation may regulate the procedure in further detail (cf. e.g. Art. 39 para. 2 AnwG/SG).

**N. 10** The duty to notify complements the right of access under → Art. 10 para. 1 lit. c BGFA, which grants cantonal supervisory authorities access to the cantonal register upon request. Whereas the right of access constitutes a reactive information instrument, the duty to notify under Art. 18 para. 2 BGFA creates a proactive obligation to provide information. The purpose of the right of access under Art. 10 BGFA is to ensure adequate intercantonal information concerning disciplinary measures (**BGE 150 II 308 E. 5.7**, with reference to BBl 1999 6013, 6045 and 6061).

---

### 4. Legal Consequences

**N. 11** Once a prohibition on practice under Art. 17 para. 1 lit. d or e BGFA in conjunction with Art. 18 para. 1 BGFA has been imposed with final effect, the person concerned is no longer authorised, for the duration of the prohibition (or permanently), to represent parties before judicial authorities within the scope of the lawyers' monopoly anywhere in Switzerland. If they nonetheless act as a lawyer, they breach Art. 12 lit. a BGFA (diligent professional conduct) and are subject to renewed disciplinary proceedings, which may in the event of repetition result in a further, more severe prohibition on practice (cf. judgment of the Administrative Court of the Canton of Bern 100.2025.299 of 27 February 2026, E. 4.1).

**N. 12** Violation of a prohibition on practice that is currently in force constitutes an independent, new breach of professional duties, to be sanctioned by the supervisory authority of the canton in which the act of representation took place (→ Art. 14 BGFA). The canton in which the lawyer is registered has jurisdiction for breaches committed before federal authorities (→ Art. 15 para. 2 BGFA; cf. judgment of the Administrative Court of the Canton of Bern 100.2025.299 of 27 February 2026, E. 3.1 ff.).

**N. 13** Cantonal rules providing for additional publication of a prohibition on practice in an official gazette are contrary to federal law pursuant to **BGE 150 II 308 E. 7.9**. The BGFA exhaustively regulates disciplinary measures; publication in an official gazette constitutes an independent repressive sanction that is not provided for in the catalogue of Art. 17 BGFA. The information necessary for enforcement of the prohibition is instead ensured through the register law system (→ Art. 5, 10 BGFA) and the duty to notify under Art. 18 para. 2 BGFA.

### 5. Disputed Questions

#### 5.1 Material Scope of Application: Monopoly Area or Comprehensive Prohibition on Practice?

**N. 14** A significant dispute exists between legal scholarship and cantonal practice regarding the material scope of the prohibition on practice within the meaning of Art. 17 para. 1 lit. d and e BGFA in conjunction with Art. 18 BGFA: does the prohibition apply only to legal activity within the scope of the lawyers' monopoly (representation of parties before courts), or also to other professionally typical activities (advisory work, negotiations, correspondence on behalf of clients)?

**N. 15** The **prevailing view in legal scholarship and case law** limits the prohibition on practice to the **monopoly area**. Poledna (Kommentar BGFA, Art. 17 N. 37) and Bauer/Bauer (CR Loi sur les avocats, 2nd ed. 2022, Art. 17 N. 70 and Art. 18 N. 1), as well as the Administrative Court of the Canton of Bern (judgment 100.2025.299 of 27 February 2026, E. 5.5), adopt this position. They rely on the wording, the legislative history (BBl 1999 6013, 6060: «Rechtsberatungen können sie vornehmen» [they may provide legal advice]), the systematic structure (Art. 2 para. 1 BGFA limits the personal scope of the Act to monopoly activities), and the teleological argument: only the monopoly activity requires state authorisation; pure advisory work is also permitted without a practising certificate and is not, as a matter of federal law, subject to disciplinary supervision (judgment of the Federal Supreme Court 2C_897/2015 of 25 May 2016 E. 7.2.1 and 7.3).

**N. 16** **Fellmann** (Anwaltsrecht, 2nd ed. 2017, N. 735 and 740), by contrast, is not unequivocal: he describes the prohibition on practice as a ban on «representation of parties before courts», which tends to correspond to the monopoly-area view, but leaves room for broader interpretations. **Brunner/Henn/Kriesi** (Anwaltsrecht, 2015, p. 251) adopt a similar position in the context of the register, without explicitly defining the material scope of the prohibition.

**N. 17** The supervisory authority of the Canton of Bern attempted in 2025 to extend the prohibition on practice to acts of representation outside the monopoly area where the breach of duty that led to the prohibition had been committed in the context of a non-monopoly mandate. The Administrative Court of the Canton of Bern expressly rejected this extension: a prohibition on practice cannot, «from a systematic legal perspective», prohibit activities that the BGFA does not regulate at all; areas not governed by federal law continue to fall within cantonal competence (judgment 100.2025.299 of 27 February 2026, E. 5.4.2). This decision has not yet been reviewed by the Federal Supreme Court.

**N. 18** The practical consequence of the prevailing view is that the prohibition on practice, despite its nationwide scope under Art. 18 para. 1 BGFA, does not have a comprehensive effect in those cantons that do not recognise a lawyers' monopoly in the field of social insurance law or in other areas. The Administrative Court of Bern qualifies this, however: a prohibition on practice nonetheless retains a considerable sanctioning effect, since it applies throughout the **entire territory of Switzerland** (Art. 18 para. 1 BGFA) and many cantons also recognise a lawyers' monopoly in social insurance law (judgment 100.2025.299 of 27 February 2026, E. 5.5).

#### 5.2 Admissibility of Publication in an Official Gazette

**N. 19** Until **BGE 150 II 308** (2024), cantonal practice and legal scholarship were divided on the question of whether cantons may, on the basis of their implementing powers, order publication of a prohibition on practice in the cantonal official gazette. The Administrative Court of St. Gallen answered this in the affirmative (judgment B 2015/6 of 23 August 2016, with reference to Art. 18 para. 2 BGFA applied by analogy), and the Cantonal Court of St. Gallen also considered notification and publication to be permissible (judgment BR.2009.1 of 19 January 2010). Cantonal scholarly opinions referred to the «pillory effect» and some regarded it as a merely informational measure.

**N. 20** The Federal Supreme Court held in **BGE 150 II 308 E. 7.9** (2024) that publication of a prohibition on practice in the cantonal official gazette constitutes an independent repressive sanction that, as an additional disciplinary measure, is not provided for in the exhaustive catalogue of Art. 17 BGFA and therefore violates the principle of federal supremacy (Art. 49 para. 1 FC). In its reasoning, the Federal Supreme Court noted that the system comprising the register entry (Art. 5 para. 2 lit. e BGFA), access to the register (Art. 10 para. 1 BGFA), and the duty to notify (Art. 18 para. 2 BGFA) is sufficient for enforcement of the prohibition (**BGE 150 II 308 E. 7.8**). Cantonal practice, notably in St. Gallen (judgment B 2024/2 of 15 August 2024), has since implemented this case law.

#### 5.3 Consideration of Deleted Disciplinary Measures in Intercantonal Relations

**N. 21** A further disputed question concerns whether disciplinary measures already deleted from the cantonal lawyers' register (→ Art. 20 BGFA: five years for warnings, reprimands, and fines; ten years after expiry for temporary prohibitions on practice) may be taken into account when assessing a new sanction — including by supervisory authorities of other cantons. Art. 18 para. 2 BGFA only governs notification of prohibitions that are currently in force; the question thus concerns the intercantonal relationship and the scope of the register law system.

**N. 22** **Brunner/Henn/Kriesi** (Anwaltsrecht, 2015, p. 251) take the view that measures no longer visible in the register must be disregarded. The Federal Supreme Court rejected this position in **BGE 150 II 308 E. 5.10** (2024) and refined its earlier case law (judgment 2C_354/2021 of 24 August 2021 E. 5.1; judgment 2C_167/2020 of 13 May 2020 E. 4.2): supervisory authorities **may** take into account prior misconduct — including sanctions deleted from the register — when assessing disciplinary measures. However, as time passes, prior measures will generally carry diminishing weight. This approach is justified by reference to the protection of the public from problematic conduct (the rehabilitation rationale in disciplinary law; **BGE 150 II 308 E. 5.8**) and to the intercantonal information mechanism of Art. 16 para. 2 BGFA (statement of the canton of registration) (**BGE 150 II 308 E. 5.9**).

### 6. Practical Notes

**N. 23** **Enforcement**: As soon as a prohibition on practice becomes final (or is immediately enforceable with suspensive effect disapplied), the imposing supervisory authority must without delay notify all cantonal supervisory authorities pursuant to Art. 18 para. 2 BGFA. Simultaneously, the register entry must be supplemented with a reference to the prohibition on practice (→ Art. 5 para. 2 lit. e BGFA). Any additional publication in an official gazette is contrary to federal law pursuant to **BGE 150 II 308** and must no longer be ordered.

**N. 24** **Jurisdiction in the event of breach of a prohibition currently in force**: If a lawyer violates the prohibition on practice imposed against them by appearing in a canton other than the canton of registration, the supervisory authority of the canton in which the act of representation took place has jurisdiction (→ Art. 14 BGFA). The supervisory authority of the canton of registration has jurisdiction only if the breach occurred before a federal authority (→ Art. 15 para. 2 BGFA). No concentration of jurisdiction in favour of the canton of registration is provided for in the BGFA (judgment of the Administrative Court of the Canton of Bern 100.2025.299 of 27 February 2026, E. 3.3, with reference to BBl 1999 6013, 6059).

**N. 25** **Intercantonal coordination**: Supervisory authorities wishing to impose a prohibition on practice must, before taking their decision, obtain a statement from the supervisory authority of the canton of registration (→ Art. 16 para. 2 BGFA). In the course of this procedure, supervisory authorities of other cantons also become aware of prior disciplinary measures, including those already deleted from the cantonal lawyers' register. These may, pursuant to **BGE 150 II 308 E. 5.9 f.**, be taken into account when assessing the sanction, but carry diminishing weight as time passes.

**N. 26** **Material scope of the prohibition — advisory mandates**: Law firms and lawyers subject to a prohibition on practice should note that, according to the prevailing view, the prohibition covers only the monopoly activity (representation of parties before courts) and not pure legal advice (BBl 1999 6013, 6060; Poledna, Kommentar BGFA, Art. 17 N. 37). Nevertheless, the person concerned may not refer to their register entry during the period of the prohibition (→ Art. 11 para. 2 BGFA). Caution is required in those cantons that also recognise a monopoly on representation outside the classical lawyers' monopoly (e.g. in social insurance law), since the prohibition would be directly enforceable in those areas.

**N. 27** **Online accessibility of the register**: In various cantons, the cantonal lawyers' registers are accessible online. The prohibition on practice appears as an annotation in the register entry and is thus publicly accessible to authorities and other authorised persons pursuant to → Art. 10 para. 1 and 2 BGFA. The Federal Supreme Court confirmed in **BGE 150 II 308 E. 7.8** that this solution — register entry combined with the duty to notify under Art. 18 para. 2 BGFA — is sufficient for enforcement of the prohibition and does not justify publication in an official gazette.
