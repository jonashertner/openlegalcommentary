# Art. 20 BGFA — Removal of Disciplinary Measures

## Doctrine

### 1. Legislative History

**N. 1** Art. 20 BGFA traces back to the Federal Council's draft bill of 28 April 1999. The explanatory message justified the removal periods by reference to the goal of harmonising the register requirements across cantons and of striking a balance between the state's interest in documenting an attorney's professional background and the rehabilitative interest of the sanctioned attorney (BBl 1999 6013, 6045 and 6061).

**N. 2** In designing the removal periods, the Federal Council was guided by the then-applicable criminal records regime, without, however, enacting a comprehensive prohibition on use analogous to former Art. 369 para. 7 SCC. The explanatory message expressly left open whether removed entries may be taken into account in subsequent disciplinary proceedings (BBl 1999 6013, 6061). The legislature thus deliberately refrained from adopting a solution equivalent to an analogous application of the prohibition on use under criminal records law within disciplinary law.

**N. 3** In Parliament, Art. 20 BGFA was debated without substantial controversy; the provision was incorporated into the statute in the form proposed by the committee. Para. 2 concerning the temporary prohibition on practising the profession was set out separately in order to reflect the greater severity of this measure relative to warnings, reprimands, and fines.

---

### 2. Systematic Classification

**N. 4** Art. 20 BGFA forms part of the fourth section on supervision (Art. 14–20 BGFA) and constitutes the register-law counterpart to Art. 17 BGFA (disciplinary measures). The provision regulates exclusively the point in time from which an already-imposed disciplinary measure must be removed from the cantonal register. It thereby supplements the register regime in Art. 5 para. 2 lit. e BGFA — under which the cantonal register contains disciplinary measures «not yet removed» — and Art. 10 BGFA, which grants access to the register data to those entitled to inspect it, in particular the cantonal supervisory authorities.

**N. 5** The provision is closely connected to Art. 17 BGFA (→ Art. 17 BGFA), which contains the exhaustive catalogue of disciplinary measures. The Federal Supreme Court has confirmed on multiple occasions that the BGFA exhaustively regulates disciplinary measures against attorneys (**BGE 150 II 308** E. 7.4; **BGE 132 II 250** E. 4.3.1; **BGE 130 II 270** E. 1.1). This pre-emptive effect of federal law does not extend to the entirety of professional regulatory law, but significantly constrains cantonal supplements (→ Art. 3 BGFA).

**N. 6** The removal periods in Art. 20 BGFA are calibrated to the individual disciplinary measures set out in Art. 17 para. 1 BGFA: para. 1 covers the three lesser measures (warning, reprimand, fine), para. 2 covers the temporary prohibition on practising the profession. The permanent prohibition on practising the profession (Art. 17 para. 1 lit. e BGFA) is not covered; it is never removed, as it permanently excludes the attorney concerned from the sphere of the professional monopoly.

---

### 3. Elements of the Provision and Normative Content

**N. 7** **Para. 1: Five-year period for lesser measures.** Warnings, reprimands, and fines are removed from the register five years after they are imposed. The period begins at the time of imposition, i.e. when the disciplinary ruling becomes final and non-appealable, and not already at the time of the conduct giving rise to the breach. This corresponds to the general rule in administrative law, under which legal consequences arise only upon the enforceability of the ruling.

**N. 8** **Para. 2: Ten-year period for the temporary prohibition on practising the profession.** A temporary prohibition on practising the profession is removed from the register ten years after its «lifting». The term «lifting» refers to the expiry of the prohibition period fixed in the operative part of the ruling, not the point in time at which the measure was imposed. It follows that, in the case of a two-year prohibition on practising the profession (the statutory maximum under Art. 17 para. 1 lit. d BGFA), the removal period begins to run only ten years after the expiry of those two years. The total period of presence in the register may thus amount to up to twelve years.

**N. 9** **Automatic removal.** The wording of the statute («shall be ... removed») indicates that removal is to be carried out ex officio, without the need for an application by the attorney concerned. The cantons are, in the exercise of their competence to maintain the register, obliged to monitor the periods and to remove entries in a timely manner (Fellmann, Anwaltsrecht, 2nd ed. 2017, para. 743; Bohnet/Martenet, Droit de la profession d'avocat, 2009, N 2060).

**N. 10** **No prohibition on use following removal.** Art. 20 BGFA does not expressly establish a prohibition on the use of removed disciplinary measures. The Federal Supreme Court clarified in **BGE 150 II 308** E. 5.7 that it cannot be inferred from the federal register regime that supervisory authorities are obliged to disregard removed measures. Art. 5 para. 2 lit. e BGFA and Art. 10 para. 1 lit. c BGFA regulate only the scope of the supervisory authorities' right to inspect the register, not the substantive question of whether entries may be used in subsequent disciplinary proceedings.

---

### 4. Legal Consequences

**N. 11** **Effect on the register.** Upon expiry of the applicable period, the disciplinary measure must be removed from the cantonal register. Accordingly, it is no longer visible to the entities entitled to inspect it (Art. 10 BGFA). The removal from the register means in particular that third parties — notably courts and authorities — can no longer obtain knowledge of the measure in question from the register.

**N. 12** **No substantive expungement.** Removal does not produce any substantive expungement in the sense of a prohibition on use. The cantonal supervisory authorities may take prior misconduct into account — including disciplinary measures already removed from the register — when determining the sanction in renewed disciplinary proceedings (**BGE 150 II 308** E. 5.10). This applies all the more since the abolition of the prohibition on use under former Art. 369 para. 7 SCC on 23 January 2023 by the Criminal Records Act (StReG; SR 330), which reflects the legislative intent to restrict the right to be forgotten (**BGE 150 II 308** E. 5.6.3).

**N. 13** **Diminishing weight with increasing lapse of time.** The Federal Supreme Court has, however, qualified the consideration of removed measures: the longer an attorney conducts themselves irreproachably following a breach, the lesser weight may be accorded to earlier disciplinary sanctions when determining a new sanction. Past disciplinary measures lose «as a rule, significance with increasing lapse of time» (**BGE 150 II 308** E. 5.8 and 5.10). This refinement of the case law preserves a practical sphere of application for the principle of rehabilitation without framing it as an absolute prohibition on use.

**N. 14** **Permanent prohibition on practising the profession.** The permanent prohibition on practising the profession under Art. 17 para. 1 lit. e BGFA is not removed. It remains permanently in the register and thus produces enduring register-law effects. This is appropriate, since the permanent prohibition on practising the profession signifies the loss of the entitlement to practise in the sphere of the professional monopoly for all time.

---

### 5. Contested Issues

**N. 15** **Dispute concerning the effect of removal (prohibition on use vs. mere register cleansing).** The central contested issue concerns the question of whether the removal of a disciplinary measure under Art. 20 BGFA gives rise to an implied prohibition on its use in subsequent disciplinary proceedings.

**N. 16** Academic commentary — to the extent it has addressed this point — has taken the position that measures no longer visible in the register must not be taken into account when determining the sanction. Brunner/Henn/Kriesi (Anwaltsrecht, 2015, p. 251) support this view by analogy with the former prohibition on use under criminal records law (former Art. 369 para. 7 SCC) and by reference to the purpose of the removal periods, which is to draw a definitive line once a rehabilitation period has elapsed.

**N. 17** By contrast, the Federal Supreme Court in **BGE 150 II 308** E. 5.7–5.10 took the opposing view and refined its earlier case law: Art. 20 BGFA contains no prohibition on use; the register-law regime governs only the content of the register, not the usability of entries outside the register. The Federal Supreme Court grounds this conclusion teleologically in the primary protective purpose of disciplinary law (protection of the public seeking legal assistance from unsuitable attorneys), which requires a comprehensive assessment of an attorney's professional background. This outcome is confirmed by the abolition of the prohibition on use under criminal records law in 2023, which removes the legislative point of reference relied upon by the opposing view.

**N. 18** The Lucerne Court of Appeal (decision of 8 July 2009, LGVE 2010 I No. 34) had, in an earlier decision, taken the view that only disciplinary measures from the preceding five years were to be taken into account when determining the sanction, deriving therefrom a restriction coextensive with the para. 1 period. This cantonal practice has been superseded by **BGE 150 II 308** and can no longer be regarded as authoritative.

**N. 19** **Dispute concerning the permissibility of cantonal publication orders.** A further contested issue arises from the relationship between Art. 20 BGFA and the principle of exhaustive federal regulation (Art. 49 para. 1 FC). The Federal Supreme Court clarified in **BGE 150 II 308** E. 7.4–7.9 that cantonal publication orders for disciplinary measures in official gazettes constitute an independent repressive sanction and are therefore incompatible with the exhaustive federal regulation. This rendered contrary to federal law the practice of publication in official cantonal gazettes exercised in certain cantons (notably Zug and St. Gallen). The St. Gallen Administrative Court expressly endorsed this case law in its judgment B 2024/2, B 2024/122 of 15 August 2024. The dispute over the permissibility of publication in official cantonal gazettes has thus been resolved by the highest court's clarification, even though not all cantons have yet amended their cantonal provisions accordingly.

**N. 20** **Dispute concerning the commencement of the period under para. 2.** Academic commentary has debated whether the ten-year period in para. 2 begins upon the expiry of the prohibition period or only upon the ruling becoming final and non-appealable. The wording of the statute («after its lifting») supports the expiry of the prohibition period. Fellmann (Anwaltsrecht, 2nd ed. 2017, para. 741) and Bohnet/Martenet (Droit de la profession d'avocat, 2009, N 2058) consistently hold that «lifting» refers to the expiry of the prohibition period fixed in the operative part of the ruling, not the lifting by means of a new ruling.

### 6. Practical Notes

**N. 21** **Register management.** The cantonal register authorities must monitor the removal periods ex officio. It is advisable to store the periods in an automated fashion in the register management system so that removals are carried out in a timely manner and without an application from the person concerned.

**N. 22** **Determination of sanctions.** Supervisory authorities are entitled to take into account also removed disciplinary measures when determining sanctions. In doing so, they must observe the principle developed in **BGE 150 II 308** E. 5.8 that the significance of earlier measures diminishes with increasing lapse of time. The reasons of the ruling must therefore explicitly set out what weight is accorded to earlier measures — including already-removed ones — in the light of the overall professional background.

**N. 23** **Inter-cantonal relations.** Supervisory authorities intending to impose a disciplinary measure on an attorney not registered in their own register are obliged under Art. 16 para. 2 BGFA to afford the supervisory authority of the register canton an opportunity to submit observations. In the course of this procedure, they may also obtain knowledge of disciplinary measures already removed in the register canton and take these into account in their assessment (**BGE 150 II 308** E. 5.9).

**N. 24** **Cantonal publication provisions.** Cantonal statutory provisions that provide for the publication of disciplinary measures in official gazettes are contrary to federal law and must not be applied (**BGE 150 II 308** E. 7.9). Affected attorneys may challenge such a publication order by way of a public-law appeal to the Federal Supreme Court.

**N. 25** **Relationship to criminal records law.** Since the entry into force of the Criminal Records Act (StReG; SR 330) on 23 January 2023, the former prohibition on use under criminal records law pursuant to former Art. 369 para. 7 SCC has been abolished. An analogy between the attorneys' register and the criminal records register that would give rise to a prohibition on the use of removed disciplinary measures is thus doubly excluded: first, for want of an express provision in the BGFA; second, because even criminal records law no longer recognises such a prohibition.
